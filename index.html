Active recall

Active Recall is a proven learning strategy that forces your brain to actively pull information out of memory—rather than passively re-reading or highlighting. Here’s how to make it work for you:

Ask Yourself Questions

Instead of re-reading notes, quiz yourself with questions.

Use Flashcards

Create flashcards with questions on one side and answers on the other.

Teach Someone Else

Explain the material out loud — to a friend or imaginary student.

Blur the Notes

Close your notes and try to recall the content from memory.

Spaced Repetition

Review information at increasing intervals to retain it longer.

Active Summarizing

Write down key ideas from memory without looking at your notes.

Practice Tests

Do tests without looking at notes, then correct your mistakes.

Recall Before Sleep

Mentally review key facts before going to bed.

Mental Mind Maps

Recreate mind maps from memory to reinforce understanding.
